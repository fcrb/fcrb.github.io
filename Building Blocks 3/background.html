A little bit of history...

The Guerrilla Girls

The Guerrilla Girls are a group of artists borne out of the rise of feminism in art movement that began to take hold in the late 70s and early 80s.

While their ambitions began directed towards the activism of fighting for representation and against discrimination for women artists and artists of color, it began to expand to a more comprehensive plethora of social agendas.

Their fervor for fighting and appetite for going against huge names in the art industry has over the decades created a huge spike in awareness and ability to hold museums and the like accountable for spreading ideals of equality and much needed change.
