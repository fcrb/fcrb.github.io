Dearest Art Collector...

“This print is unusual in the portfolio in that it takes the form of an enlarged handwritten letter. It is addressed to an anonymous ‘Dearest Art Collector’ and simply states the Guerilla Girls’ confidence in the collector’s intention to collect more art by women. It is written in curly, girly handwriting on a pink ground; a drawing of a flower with a sad face heads it while the Girls’ PO Box address is printed at the foot. It was originally made in 1986” (Tate.org.uk).

Manchester, Elizabeth. “'Dearest Art Collector', Guerrilla Girls, 1986.” Tate, January 1, 1986. https://www.tate.org.uk/art/artworks/guerrilla-girls-dearest-art-collector-p78802.
